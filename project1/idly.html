Idly recipe

Ingredients: For the Idly Batter: 2 cups of parboiled rice 1 cup of whole urad dal (black gram) 1/2 teaspoon of fenugreek seeds Water for soaking and grinding Salt to taste For Steaming Idly: 6. Idly molds or plates Water for steaming
Instructions: Preparing the Idly Batter: In a large bowl, wash and soak the parboiled rice and fenugreek seeds together. In a separate bowl, wash and soak the urad dal. Soak both the rice and urad dal separately for at least 4-6 hours, or overnight for best results. After soaking, drain the water from both the rice and urad dal. Grinding the Idly Batter: 4. Using a wet grinder or a high-speed blender, grind the soaked urad dal into a smooth and fluffy batter. Add water as needed to achieve this consistency. Similarly, grind the soaked rice and fenugreek seeds into a smooth batter. The rice batter should be slightly coarse compared to the urad dal batter. Combine both batters in a large bowl. Add salt and mix well. The batter should be thick but with a pouring consistency. Cover the bowl and let it ferment for 8-12 hours or overnight. Steaming the Idly: 8. Before steaming, mix the fermented batter gently. If it's too thick, you can add a little water to achieve the right consistency. Grease the idly molds or plates with a little oil or use non-stick molds. Pour the batter into each mold, filling them about 3/4 full. In a steamer, add water and bring it to a boil. Place the idly molds in the steamer and cover it with a lid. Steam the idlies for 10-12 minutes on medium heat, or until a toothpick inserted into an idly comes out clean. Once done, remove the molds from the steamer and let them cool for a few minutes. Use a wet spoon to gently remove the idlies from the molds. Serving the Idly: 16. Serve hot idlies with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, sambar, or any other favorite accompaniment. Enjoy your homemade soft and fluffy idlies! Note: The fermentation time may vary depending on the temperature of your kitchen. The batter should double in volume after fermenting, and it should have a slightly tangy flavor.
